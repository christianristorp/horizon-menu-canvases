Methodology

What this measures. Every row is a customer segment. The six region percentages show what share of that segment's recipe selections (individual recipes chosen into a box, not customers and not boxes) fell into each recipe region. Percentages are within-segment and sum to 100% across the six regions in each row.Selection universe. Recipe selections from facts_recipes_ordered_enriched (column recipe_region), joined to the segmentation table on customer + country. Scope: DK + SE, weeks 2025-W31 to 2025-W52 (the H2-2025 segmentation window), Standard core recipes only (slot_type = Recipe, excluding Modular Recipes), counting only recipes the customer actively kept or swapped in. One row in the source = one recipe in one customer's box in one week.Regions. The six raw recipe_region values are shown as-is with no grouping: European, Asian, American, Fusion/Other, Middle Eastern, African. Columns are ordered by overall selection volume descending.Customer share (two definitions). "Cust. share" = distinct customers in that segment as a % of the total DKSE base (43,840). At Layer 3 a second column, "Reach (roll-up)", sums the collapsed-segment shares that contain each main segment — e.g. Family reach = Family+Convenience (40.3%) + Family+Healthy (18.2%) + Family (9.6%) = 68.0%. Reach double-counts customers in overlapping segments, so it shows total addressable reach rather than an exclusive share.Over/under-index. Below each layer, a heatmap divides the segment's region share by the menu-wide average share for that region. 1.00 = on par with the menu; above 1 over-indexes (green), below 1 under-indexes (orange). Menu-wide averages: European 41.4%, Asian 24.9%, American 18.6%, Fusion/Other 11.0%, Middle Eastern 3.2%, African 0.9% (across all 1,726,147 segmented selections).

The three layers

Layer 3 — Overarching (main segment). Customers grouped by main segment, with Vegetarian + Pescatarian merged into Veg/Pesc. The region percentages here are customer-weighted across the collapsed segments that roll up into each main segment, so they correlate with the reach figure rather than being a separately computed aggregate.Layer 2 — Collapsed (7 segments). The planning grain. Overlapping pairs are merged regardless of order (Family+Convenience = Convenience+Family). Each customer appears once, so customer shares sum to ~100%. Other is shown for reference but is excluded from menu slot allocation.Layer 1 — Super-detailed (main x secondary). The raw segment pairs before collapsing. "None" means no secondary segment. Pescatarian and Vegetarian are shown separately here (not yet merged into Veg/Pesc). Each customer appears once.
