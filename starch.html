Methodology

What this measures. Every row is a customer segment. The nine starch percentages show what share of that segment's recipe selections (individual recipes chosen into a box, not customers and not boxes) fell into each starch group. Percentages are within-segment and sum to 100% across the nine groups in each row.Selection universe. Recipe selections from facts_recipes_ordered_enriched, joined to the segmentation table on customer + country. Scope: DK + SE, weeks 2025-W31 to 2025-W52 (the H2-2025 segmentation window), Standard core recipes only (slot_type = Recipe, excluding Modular Recipes), counting only recipes the customer actively kept or swapped in. One row in the source = one recipe in one customer's box in one week.Starch grouping. The 13 raw categories collapse to 9: Bread = Bread + Dough + Panko Breadcrumbs; No starch = NULL + None + No starch; Potato, Rice, Pasta, Tortilla, Grains, Noodles and Couscous are unchanged.Customer share (two definitions). "Cust. share" = distinct customers in that segment as a % of the total DKSE base (43,840). At Layer 3 a second column, "Reach (roll-up)", sums the collapsed-segment shares that contain each main segment — e.g. Family reach = Family+Convenience (40.3%) + Family+Healthy (18.2%) + Family (9.6%) = 68.0%. Reach double-counts customers in overlapping segments.

The three layers

Layer 3 — Overarching (main segment). Customers grouped by main segment, with Vegetarian + Pescatarian merged into Veg/Pesc. Starch percentages here are customer-weighted across the collapsed segments that roll up into each main segment, so they correlate with the reach figure.Layer 2 — Collapsed (7 segments). The planning grain. Overlapping pairs merged regardless of order. Each customer appears once. Other shown for reference, excluded from slot allocation.Layer 1 — Super-detailed (main x secondary). Raw segment pairs before collapsing. "None" = no secondary segment. Pescatarian and Vegetarian shown separately (not yet merged).
