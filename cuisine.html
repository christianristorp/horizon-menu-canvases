Cuisine Penetration by Segment

How often each customer segment's actual recipe selections are tagged with each national cuisine. Cuisines overlap (one recipe can carry several), so values are independent penetration rates and do not sum to 100%. Read top-down: the overarching view is the big picture, the collapsed view is the 7-segment planning grain, and the detailed view exposes the raw segment pairs.

Methodology

What this measures. Every row is a customer segment. Each cuisine percentage is the penetration: the share of that segment's recipe selections (individual recipes chosen into a box) tagged with the cuisine. Because the cuisines field is an array and a recipe can carry several cuisines, penetrations are independent and do not sum to 100%.Selection universe. Recipe selections from facts_recipes_ordered_enriched (array column cuisines, exploded), joined to the segmentation table on customer + country. Scope: DK + SE, weeks 2025-W31 to 2025-W52, Standard core recipes only (slot_type = Recipe, excluding Modular Recipes), counting only recipes actively kept or swapped in. 1,726,147 segmented selections. Roughly a quarter of selections carry no cuisine tag, so penetrations across all cuisines sum to well under 100%.Cuisines. All 29 national cuisines present in the data are shown, ordered by overall penetration descending (Italian and Scandinavian dominate; a long tail runs down to Peruvian).Over/under-index. Below each layer, a heatmap divides the segment's cuisine penetration by the menu-wide average penetration for that cuisine. 1.0 = on par with the menu; above 1 over-indexes (green), below 1 under-indexes (orange).Customer share. "Cust. share" = distinct customers in that segment as a % of the total DKSE base (43,840). At Layer 3 a second figure, "reach", sums the collapsed-segment shares that contain each main segment (double-counts overlap customers, so it shows total reach).

Layer 3 — Overarching (main segment)

Customer-weighted across the collapsed segments rolling up into each main segment. Cust. share = exclusive distinct; reach = additive across overlapping segments.
