Methodology

What this measures. Every row is a customer segment. The dish type percentages show what share of that segment's recipe selections (individual recipes chosen into a box, not customers and not boxes) fell into each dish type. Percentages are within-segment and sum to 100% across the 25 dish types in each row.Selection universe. Recipe selections from facts_recipes_ordered_enriched, joined to the segmentation table on customer + country. Scope: DK + SE, weeks 2025-W31 to 2025-W52 (the H2-2025 segmentation window), Standard core recipes only (slot_type = Recipe, excluding Modular Recipes), counting only recipes the customer actively kept or swapped in. One row in the source = one recipe in one customer's box in one week.Dish types. All 25 raw dish_type values are shown individually with no grouping. "123" is an explicit dish type in the source and is shown verbatim. Columns are ordered by overall selection volume descending.Customer share (two definitions). "Cust. share" = distinct customers in that segment as a % of the total DKSE base (43,840). At Layer 3 a second column, "Reach (roll-up)", sums the collapsed-segment shares that contain each main segment — e.g. Family reach = Family+Convenience (40.3%) + Family+Healthy (18.2%) + Family (9.6%) = 68.0%. Reach double-counts customers in overlapping segments, so it shows total addressable reach rather than an exclusive share.Over/under-index. Below each layer, a heatmap divides the segment's dish-type share by the menu-wide average share for that dish type. 1.00 = on par with the menu; above 1 over-indexes (green), below 1 under-indexes (orange). Menu-wide averages: 123 18.1%, Pasta 13.5%, Bowl 13.3%, Crumbed 8.0%, Meatballs 8.0%, Tacos 5.1%, Noodles 4.5%, Stir-Fry 3.5%, Burger 3.3%, Salad 3.1%, One-Pot 2.9%, Stew 2.7%, Gratin 2.6%, Soup 2.2%, One-Tray 1.9%, Curry 1.5%, Sandwich 1.4%, Wrap 1.0%, Fritters 0.9%, Risotto 0.8%, Pizza 0.8%, Pie 0.4%, Nachos 0.3%, Flatbread 0.2%, Tortillas 0.1% (across all 1,726,147 segmented selections).

The three layers

Layer 3 — Overarching (main segment). Customers grouped by main segment, with Vegetarian + Pescatarian merged into Veg/Pesc. The dish type percentages here are customer-weighted across the collapsed segments that roll up into each main segment, so they correlate with the reach figure rather than being a separately computed aggregate.Layer 2 — Collapsed (7 segments). The planning grain. Overlapping pairs are merged regardless of order (Family+Convenience = Convenience+Family). Each customer appears once, so customer shares sum to ~100%. Other is shown for reference but is excluded from menu slot allocation.Layer 1 — Super-detailed (main x secondary). The raw segment pairs before collapsing. "None" means no secondary segment. Pescatarian and Vegetarian are shown separately here (not yet merged into Veg/Pesc). Each customer appears once.

Layer 3 — Overarching (main segment)

Cust. share = exclusive distinct customers. Reach (roll-up) = additive across overlapping segments (double-counts). Dish type % is customer-weighted from the collapsed segments.
